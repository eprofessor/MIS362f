Introduction to Information Systems



700 Points
Learning Objectives

These exercises introduce basic basic Information Systems concepts.

After completing this module you will be able to:

  1.  Distinguish data from information and knowledge (CLO 1).
  2.  Describe the characteristics of quality data (CLO 1).
  3.  Describe the functions of information system components (CLO 1).
  4.  Explain the different types of information systems (CLO 1).
  5.  Describe the role of the components of an information technology infrastructure (CLO 1).
  6.  Describe how organizations are using business intelligence and business analytics (CLO 1).
  7.  Discuss why it is critical for business objectives and IS activities to be well aligned through system planning, development, and acquisition (CLO 1)
  8.  Identify major IT security threats, and legal, social, and ethical issues associated with information systems (CLO 1).
  9.  Create queries in Access 2016 using Query-by-Example (CLO 11).
Assignment Overview

The goal of this assignment is to enable you to work with data and information.
You are going to develop a working knowledge of information systems during this course, and discussing the concepts is a great way to do this.
If you are having trouble there are several resources available, besides a Google Search
You can also ask a teaching assistant or ask the instructor for assistance.

  1. Working with others
    1. Read the first Chapters of the book.
    2. Think about the concepts.
    3. Discuss these concepts with a partner.
    4. Reflect on the effect these concepts have on your partner.
  2. Information Systems Impact
    1. Discuss the impact that increasingly large amounts of data has on available technology.
    2. Discuss the social impact of large amounts of data.
  3. The bigger picture.
    1. Familiarize yourself with the Internet of Things
    2. Investigate a company dealing the IoT
    3. Investigate an impact of IoT and the large data sets it generates.
