Organizations and Information Systems



600 Points
Learning Objectives

These exercises introduce structures and management concepts used to define how organizations use information systems.

After completing this module you will be able to:

  1.  Describe change models that increases the likelihood of successfully introducing a new information system in an organization (CLO 2).
  2.  Explain reengineering and continuous improvement (CLO 2).
  3. Show how information systems support and work within the automated portions of an organizational process (CLO 1).
  4. Define the types of roles, functions, and careers available in the field of information systems (CLO 1).
  5. Explain value chain with examples (CLO 1).
  6.  Discuss pros and cons of outsourcing, offshoring, and downsizing (CLO 2)
  7.  Explain two types of innovations (CLO 1).
  8.  Explain how soft-side implementation is a critical factor in the successful adoption of any major change (CLO 2).
  9.  Create a database and tables in Access 2016 (CLO 11).
