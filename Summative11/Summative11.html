Strategic Planning and Project Management



550 Points
Learning Objectives

These exercises introduce concepts used to define how organizations incorporate strategic planning and project management into information systems.

After completing this module you will be able to:

  1. State the benefits of creating a strategic plan
  2.  Identify and briefly describe the phases of a goals-based strategic planning process
  3. Explain what SWOT (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats) analysis is and how it is used
  4.  Identify and briefly describe the components of a strategic plan
  5.  State the purpose of the IS strategic plan
  6.  Identify four drivers that set information system organizational strategy and determine information system investments
  7.  Identify three ways the IS organization can be perceived by the rest of the organization and discuss how that influences the IS strategy
  8.  Discuss why project management is considered to be a core competency for many organizations
  9.  Identify and briefly discuss the five highly interrelated parameters that define a project
  10.  Identify and briefly discuss the nine knowledge areas associated with the science of project management
